Idea Wall

“People think focus means saying yes to the thing you've got to focus on. But that's not what it means at all. It means saying no to the hundred other good ideas that there are. You have to pick carefully. I'm actually as proud of the things we haven't done as the things I have done. Innovation is saying no to 1,000 things.” —Steve Jobs

In the spirit of our current strategic discussions, we thought it’d be cathartic to look at the possibilities we’ve discussed this past year. This wall displays all the ideas we could find. See how things come up repeatedly; see how things relate; see what people love; see where things don’t fall into one category.

We’re not on all the mailing lists, so we know that we've missed tonnes. Add ideas yourself using physical stickies! Excited about something? Had that idea yourself? Put your initials on it! Add sketches or notes!

As part of Research, we want to explore everything from specific content (i.e. “Substance”) to how we work with each other (i.e. “How we work”). This poster is an experiment in the latter. Email us your thoughts or scribble feelings here.

Want to follow the links you see? http://khanacademy.org/r/ideawall.

—May-Li & Andy

Substance

The “what” itself, e.g.
  • Actual content
  • Region-specific things
  • Standardized tests we support

Form

Structures around the “what”, e.g.
  • Mediums for presenting instruction
  • Arrangement or navigation of content
  • New interactions

Varying Approaches

Asking “why” more deeply, e.g.
  • Creatively reframing problems
  • Digging into the roots of problems
  • Empowering others to do the thing

Assessment

Better metrics—for students and for us, e.g.
  • Replacing report cards, tests, credentials
  • Measuring social-emotional outcomes
  • Alternate measures of effectiveness

How We Work

Like introspection, but for an organization, e.g.
  • Organizational design
  • Innovations in how we work
  • Improving our internal processes
